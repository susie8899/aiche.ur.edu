Following are some resources for finding research oppoturnities:

  • Research Experiences for Undergraduates (REU) is an opportunity to do research at a college or university often throughout the summer. Many are funded by the National Science Foundation (NSF) and allow students to explore labs beyond those at the University of Rochesterâ€™s Chemical Engineering Department.
  • The Eisenberg Summer Research Fellowship provides funding for research advancements in the Chemical Engineering Department. Chemical Engineering Students in their sophomore and junior years can apply to work in the projects included that year.
  • The Schwartz Discover Grant is another opportunity for students to receive funding to work on a project over the summer. Unlike the Eisenberg Fellowship, its application is open to students not only in Chemical Engineering, but also in the School of Arts & Sciences and the Hajim School of Engineering.
  • If you have any further question regarding research applications, fellowships, and research grants, the Office of Undergraduates Research will be a great resource.
